Letter to Arc Members 2025

On Arc, its future, and the arrival of AI browsers — answering the largest questions from the past year.

1

Dear Arc Members

From the outside, the pivot to Dia might look abrupt. Inside, the decision was slower and more deliberate.

  • Why we started this company
  • What Arc taught us
  • What happens to Arc now
  • Why we believe Dia is the next step

2

What We Got Wrong

Three things we'd do differently if we could start over:

  • Stop working on Arc a year earlier — we saw the data on growth and retention but were in denial
  • Embrace AI fully, sooner — personal curiosity was real, but industry hype made us pull back
  • Communicate very differently — sometimes too transparent, sometimes not transparent enough

Note

"The truth will set you free." — a mentor's advice that should have been followed more.

3

Arc Explore: An Early Signal

Our Act II video (January 2024) ended with a prototype called Arc Explore — an idea that is basically where Dia and other AI-native products are headed now.

Our instincts were there long before our hearts caught up.

4

Why We Built Arc

A simple belief: the browser is the most important software in your life — and it wasn't getting the attention it deserved.

  • Everything was moving into the browser by 2019
  • Cloud revenue surging, breakout startups were browser-based
  • The dominant desktop OS wasn't Windows or macOS — it was the browser
  • Chrome and Safari hadn't evolved with the shift

We wanted to build "your home on the internet" — more Nintendo or Disney than browser vendor.

5

Where Arc Fell Short: The Novelty Tax

Arc was too different, with too many new things to learn, for too little reward.

  • D1 retention was strong — those who stuck became fanatics
  • But metrics looked like a specialized pro tool, not a mass-market product
  • Arc lacked cohesion in both features and core value
  • Switching browsers is a big ask for incremental benefit

6

Where Arc Fell Short: The Data

What people actually used differs from what the average tweet assumes:

  • Only 5.52% of DAUs use more than one Space regularly
  • Only 4.17% use Live Folders
  • Only 0.4% use Calendar Preview on Hover
  • By contrast, Dia's chat-with-tabs: 40% of DAUs
  • Dia's personalization features: 37% of DAUs

7

Where Arc Fell Short: Too Incremental

The hard truth: Arc and even Arc Search were too incremental for our original aspirations.

  • In 2023, ChatGPT and Perplexity threatened Google
  • Cursor reshaped the IDE
  • Users who'd done things the same way for decades were suddenly open to change
  • This was the moment we were waiting for

If we were serious about our original mission, we needed a technological unlock to build something truly new.

8

Why We Didn't Integrate Dia into Arc

Dia was an opportunity to fix what we got wrong with Arc:

  • Simplicity over novelty — Scott Forstall challenged us: make it a piano, not a saxophone
  • Speed as foundation — fresh architecture, sunsetting TCA and SwiftUI, prioritizing performance
  • Security at the forefront — grew security team from 1 to 5; red teaming, bug bounties, internal audits

These need to be part of a product's foundation, not afterthoughts.

9

Will We Open Source Arc?

We still maintain Arc — Chromium upgrades, security fixes, bug fixes. But we're not actively developing new features.

The complication: Arc runs on ADK (Arc Development Kit), our internal SDK for building browsers with imaginative interfaces.

  • ADK lets ex-iOS engineers prototype native browser UI without touching C++
  • ADK is also the foundation of Dia
  • Open-sourcing Arc meaningfully requires open-sourcing ADK
  • ADK is still core to our company's value

If the day comes where it no longer puts our team or shareholders at risk, we'd be excited to share it. But we're not there yet.

10

Building Dia: The Core Belief

Traditional browsers, as we know them, will die — much like search engines and IDEs are being reimagined.

  • We're not stopping searching or coding — the environments will look different
  • The Browser Company is still making browsers — but an AI browser is different from a Web browser
  • We're getting out of the candle business at the dawn of electric light

11

Three Ways AI Browsers Differ

1. Webpages won't be the primary interface anymore - Apps, articles, files will become tool calls with AI chat interfaces - Chat interfaces already search, read, generate, respond, interact with APIs - Natural language interfaces are here to stay

2. The Web isn't going anywhere - Figma, NYT, SaaS tools remain essential - Tabs are our core context, not expendable - The most powerful interface will be both web browser and AI chat — like peanut butter and jelly

3. New interfaces start from familiar ones - Computing is changing faster than most acknowledge - Yet we're farther from abandoning old ways than AI insiders think - Cursor proved it: the breakthrough AI app was an (old) IDE, designed to be AI-native

12

Your Home on the Internet

The Browser Company assembled for the chance — however slim — to build something that rewired how we use computers.

  • Dia may not be your style. It may not land right away.
  • But this is still us — being ourselves, building what we'd want to use.
  • Fully aware we might be wrong. But doing it anyway.
  • Five years from now, the most-used AI interfaces on desktop will replace the default browsers of yesteryear.

This is our truth, and we sincerely hope that you'll like what comes next. — Josh
